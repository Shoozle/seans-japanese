Kanji

Kanji characters are adapted from Chinese letters and are used to represent something. The character that follows the Kanji can change how the Kanji is pronounced. There is On-yomi Kanji which is pronounced the Chinese way, and Kun-yomi which is pronounced the Japanese way. To help study Kanji, you can look at Kanji as being made up of 'primitives' which are blocks of caligraphy used repeatedly to make up other Kanji. For example, 人 and 大 meaning じん (person) and だい (large).
